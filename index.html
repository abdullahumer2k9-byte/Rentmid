The gap in the market

Two bad options, until now

Life at 20–200 units today

  • Consumer apps (built for 1–10 units) stop scaling the moment you cross a few dozen doors.
  • Enterprise platforms like Yardi, AppFolio, and RealPage are built for 500+ units — with enterprise pricing, sales calls, and onboarding to match.
  • Rent gets tracked in spreadsheets, maintenance in text threads, leases in a filing cabinet or a shared drive.
  • State-by-state landlord-tenant law — deposits, rent control, Fair Housing — gets tracked manually, if at all.

Life on RentMid

  • One system for rent collection, maintenance, leases, tenant communication, and reporting.
  • Per-unit pricing that scales with your portfolio — no enterprise contracts, no onboarding fees.
  • State-aware compliance built in from the launch state, expanding state by state rather than claiming nationwide coverage on day one.
  • Set up in an afternoon, not a quarter-long implementation.
Everything in one place

Core features

Six systems mid-sized landlords currently duct-tape together — replaced with one.

Rent collection & payments

Automated ACH and card collection, payment reminders, late-fee automation, and a full payment history per tenant and property.

Lease & document management

Digital lease storage with renewal tracking, state-aware templates and clauses, and secure storage for applications and correspondence.

Maintenance request tracking

Tenant-facing portal with photo/video upload, a manager dashboard to assign and prioritize, and a timestamped history for liability protection.

Tenant communication portal

Centralized messaging that replaces scattered texts and emails, plus automated notices for rent due, maintenance, and renewals.

Financial reporting

Per-property and portfolio-level income and expense reports, exportable for accounting, tax filing, and Schedule E preparation.

Tenant screening

Background, credit, and eviction history checks via integrated third-party screening, plus digital applications with automated workflow.
