The gap

Two kinds of software. Neither fits the middle.

Consumer tools like Avail or TurboTenant are built for landlords with a handful of doors. Enterprise platforms like Yardi, AppFolio, and RealPage are built for portfolios in the thousands, with the pricing and complexity to match. If you manage 20 to 200 units, you're stuck outgrowing one and overpaying for the other.

Consumer apps

Built for 1–10 units. Fine at first, but no room to grow into multi-property operations or team workflows.

RentMid

Purpose-built for 20–200 units — the exact range where spreadsheets break and enterprise software is overkill.

Everything in one place

Six jobs. One system.

The daily work of running a mid-sized portfolio, without switching between six different tools.

Rent Collection & Payments

ACH and card collection, automatic reminders, late-fee automation, and a full payment history per tenant and property.

Lease & Document Management

Digital lease storage with renewal tracking, state-aware templates, and secure storage for applications and correspondence.

Maintenance Request Tracking

Tenants submit requests with photos, managers assign and prioritize, and every update is timestamped for liability protection.

Tenant Communication Portal

One thread replaces scattered texts and email. Automated notices for rent due, maintenance updates, and lease renewals.

Financial Reporting

Per-property and portfolio-level income and expense reports, exportable for accounting, tax filing, and Schedule E.

Tenant Screening

Background, credit, and eviction history checks via third-party integration, plus digital applications with automated workflow.
