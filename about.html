The missing middle of property management

Property management software has a gap in it — and it happens to be exactly where most working landlords live.

Somewhere between a landlord renting out a duplex and a management company running a thousand-unit portfolio, there's an enormous, unglamorous middle: independent landlords and small-to-mid-sized management companies running 20 to 200 residential units, usually in a single state.

Too big for consumer tools

These are operators who've outgrown a shared inbox and a spreadsheet, but they don't need — or want to pay for — a system built for a portfolio ten times their size. Consumer-grade tools are built for 1–10 units. Past that, workflows built for a handful of doors start to break: no real maintenance tracking, no meaningful financial reporting, no help with the state-specific rules that come with running a real business.

Too small for enterprise platforms

On the other end, platforms like Yardi, AppFolio, and RealPage are built for portfolios of 500 units and up. They come with enterprise pricing, sales calls, and onboarding complexity that make sense at that scale — and very little sense for a landlord running 60 units on their own.

A large market, still underserved

This isn't a small niche. Globally, more than 41 million commercial and residential properties are managed using property management software, and the United States is the largest regional market for that software, generating close to a billion dollars in annual revenue. Yet the mid-sized segment — 20 to 200 units — remains the part of that market both ends of the industry have left behind.

Built for how US landlords actually operate

US property management is also genuinely complicated in ways that vary by location: rent control rules, eviction procedures, security deposit limits, and Fair Housing Act compliance all differ, or apply differently, depending on the state and city. RentMid is designed to expand state by state, starting with a single launch state, rather than claiming nationwide compliance on day one — because landlord-tenant law in the US is set at the state, and sometimes city, level.

Key differentiator

Unlike generic property tools or enterprise platforms scaled down for smaller clients, RentMid is built from the ground up for the operational and legal needs of mid-sized US property managers — combining simplicity, fair per-unit pricing, and state-law-aware compliance from day one.
