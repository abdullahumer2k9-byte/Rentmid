The missing middle

Somewhere between a spreadsheet and Yardi, there's a gap. That's where we live.

Property management software has a size problem. RentMid exists to fix it for the landlords stuck in the middle.

01 — too small for enterprise

Enterprise platforms are built for 500+ units

Yardi, AppFolio, and RealPage are excellent tools — for portfolios that dwarf a mid-sized landlord's. Their pricing, sales process, and feature depth assume a management company running thousands of doors. A 60-unit landlord signing up gets a system built for a problem five times their size, at a price to match.

02 — too big for consumer tools

Consumer apps are built for 1–10 units

Tools like Avail or TurboTenant are genuinely good for someone renting out a house or two. But once a landlord is running 20, 50, or 100 units, those tools start to show their seams — no team workflows, no portfolio-level reporting, no room to operate like the business it's become.

03 — the gap in the middle

20 to 200 units is a real, sizable market that's currently underserved

Globally, over 41 million commercial and residential properties are managed using property management software, and the U.S. is the single largest regional market for that software, generating close to a billion dollars in annual revenue. Yet the mid-sized segment — landlords and management companies with 20 to 200 units — remains poorly served, forced to either outgrow simple consumer apps or overpay for systems built for portfolios far larger than theirs.

Our approach

Purpose-built, not retrofitted

RentMid isn't an enterprise product with features hidden, or a consumer app with more buttons bolted on. It's designed from the ground up around the operational and legal reality of managing 20–200 units in the United States.

Simplicity

One system for rent, leases, maintenance, communication, and reporting — no six-tab workflow to keep a portfolio running.

Fair pricing

Per-unit pricing that scales naturally with portfolio size, without enterprise-style contracts or sales calls to find out the cost.

State-law-aware compliance

Security deposit limits, eviction procedures, and Fair Housing Act considerations built in from day one, launch state first.
